“The character A is enclosed above and below by marks like a carpenter’s square — what are they for?
May I omit them if I wish?”

He replied:
“No, you may not. Those are quotation marks.

‘Single quotes’ distinguish variables from other things.
‘Double quotes’ distinguish spoken phrases from other things.
Without them, one could not avoid ambiguity.”

He asked again:
“What of punctuation, and spacing — may I do without them?”
