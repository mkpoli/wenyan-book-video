was first devised by the people of the West.
Thus, its terms must be translated into our tongue.
Much of its diction was unknown in antiquity;
and even where the characters are the same,
the meanings differ.
Therefore, before speaking of programming itself,
one must first make plain the words it employs.

Once Paul Siu translated The Elements,
he first clarified the terminology of geometry —
such is the proper way.

One must not, like the ‘river chariots’ and ‘vermillion birds’ in Baopuzi,
bewilder the learner with arcane words, leaving the reader unsure of what is being said.

Let us, then, begin to unfold our explanation.
