“…was first devised by the people of the West.
Thus, its terms must be rendered into our tongue through translation.
Many of its expressions are ancient to them, yet unheard by us;
some characters may be the same, but their meanings differ.

Therefore, before speaking of programming itself,
one must first explain the words it employs.

In former times, when Xu Guangqi translated The Elements of Geometry,
he first clarified the terminology of geometry —
such is the proper way.

One must not, like the alchemists who speak of ‘river chariots’ and ‘vermillion birds,’
confuse the learner with arcane words,
so that the reader knows not what is being said.

Let us, then, begin to unfold our explanation.”
