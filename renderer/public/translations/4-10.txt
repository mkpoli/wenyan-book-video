Some asked,
“There are a thousand ends to affairs.
Not a single plan can determine it.
What then?”
It is answered, “
We ought to employ “or if.”
“Or if，” —
After the first “if.”
Continue to apply it.
Compare by order.
When truth is met, act upon it.
Thus do, and cease.
No further comparison.
If all are not so.
Then, turn to the last line of “if not,”
Lao Tzu says, Heaven and Earth are not benevolent.
And treat all things as straw-dogs.
